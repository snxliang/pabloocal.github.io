I grew up tracing my father's boot-prints across alpine meadow-grass, muddy banks of glacial tarns, and loose rock broken up by the freeze of countless winters and the thaw of just as many springs. We would spend days and weeks above the treeline. We traversed rocky rollercoaster ridgelines stalking faraway herds of stone sheep who leapt so gracefully along the mountains' steep slopes that we could only clumsily scramble across.

Wispy skies and damp moss gave away the early spring. Dad passed me the spotting scope.

"Do you see that part that looks like a nose?"

"No."

"It's below the green patch."

"Which one?"

"The big one."

"They're all pretty big."
